WHAT IS KNN?
K-Nearest Neighbours (KNN) is a simple but powerful algorithm: to classify
or recommend something, look at its K closest neighbours in a dataset and
let them "vote." No training phase — just distance calculations at query time.

The core question is always: what does "close" mean?
- For numbers, we use Euclidean distance
- For ratings, cosine similarity works well
- For text, there are many options
Netflix uses a high-dimensional version of this idea.


HOW NETFLIX USES KNN
Each user is represented as a vector of ratings — one number per movie.
Example: If you rated Inception 5/5 and Interstellar 4/5, your vector
looks like [5, 4, ...]

Netflix finds users whose vectors are close to yours (your K nearest
neighbours), then recommends movies those neighbours loved but you
haven't seen yet.


THE DISTANCE FORMULA
KNN computes the Euclidean distance between two users' rating vectors.
For users A and B with ratings on N movies:

  distance(A, B) = sqrt( sum of (A_i - B_i)^2 )

A smaller distance = more similar taste = higher priority neighbour.


SIMILARITY EXAMPLE — RATINGS TABLE
User     | Inception | Interstellar | Parasite | The Crown
---------|-----------|--------------|----------|-----------
Arjun    |     5     |      5       |    2     |     1
Priya    |     4     |      5       |    3     |     2
Sam      |     2     |      1       |    5     |     5
Mei      |     3     |      3       |    4     |     4
Carlos   |     5     |      4       |    1     |     2


DISTANCE CALCULATIONS (example: target = Arjun)
---------------------------------------------
Arjun vs Priya:
  sqrt((5-4)^2 + (5-5)^2 + (2-3)^2 + (1-2)^2)
  = sqrt(1 + 0 + 1 + 1)
  = sqrt(3)
  ≈ 1.73   <-- closest neighbour

Arjun vs Carlos:
  sqrt((5-5)^2 + (5-4)^2 + (2-1)^2 + (1-2)^2)
  = sqrt(0 + 1 + 1 + 1)
  = sqrt(3)
  ≈ 1.73   <-- equally close

Arjun vs Mei:
  sqrt((5-3)^2 + (5-3)^2 + (2-4)^2 + (1-4)^2)
  = sqrt(4 + 4 + 4 + 9)
  = sqrt(21)
  ≈ 4.58

Arjun vs Sam:
  sqrt((5-2)^2 + (5-1)^2 + (2-5)^2 + (1-5)^2)
  = sqrt(9 + 16 + 9 + 16)
  = sqrt(50)
  ≈ 7.07   <-- farthest


RESULT FOR K=2 (Arjun's nearest neighbours)
  Rank 1: Priya  (distance ≈ 1.73)
  Rank 2: Carlos (distance ≈ 1.73)

Recommendation: Movies that Priya and Carlos rated highly
but Arjun hasn't watched yet would be recommended to Arjun.


HOW K AFFECTS RESULTS
- Small K (1-2): Very personalised but noisy recommendations
- Large K:       Smoother results but may dilute relevance
- Netflix uses large K with additional weighting tricks


LIMITATIONS OF KNN IN PRACTICE
- Slow at scale (Netflix has hundreds of millions of users)
- Pure KNN requires comparing against every user — O(n) per query
- Real systems use:
    * Approximate Nearest Neighbour (ANN) methods
    * Dimensionality reduction (SVD, matrix factorisation)
    * Clustering to pre-group similar users
